Becoming a GPA Heavyweight

No heavyweight boxing champion got there by simply stepping into the ring. Getting to be a true heavyweight took training, practice, and focus. The same is true of becoming a GPA heavyweight. There is no magic formula for getting better grades. To improve your GPA, you must prepare, focus, and work hard.

You also have to make sure that you prepare and work correctly, however. Simply going to a library for a few hours won’t do. In fact, if you find a library with really comfortable chairs, you might just sleep and not do any work. In order to guarantee that you will be a GPA Heavyweight, you have to plan, study in the right environment, and use your time wisely.

Lectures

Taking notes during a class lecture, or any other kind of presentation, can be a trap. The temptation when taking notes is to write down everything you hear. This temptation must be avoided at all costs. Imagine you have an English teacher who introduces Jane Austen’s Pride and Prejudice with this brief information.

The first step in great note taking is to come up with a personal shorthand: abbreviations, consolidations, and annotations that consolidate information in a way that makes sense to you. The real key is to find the big information and create an appropriate shorthand. The two paragraphs of lecture can look like this:

  • Jane Austen
    • b. 1775
    • Daught of Angl min
    • Largely self-taught
    • Began writ 1790s
    • 1st Novel: Sense and Sensibility (1811)
  • Pride and Prejudice (PP)
    • Publ 1813
    • Bennett Sisters courtship
    • Eliz Bennett--Mr. Darcy & Mr. Wickham
    • Free Indirect Speech--third person narration w/ character’s own manners and thoughts
